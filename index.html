The Story

In 2019, Bengaluru was the world's most traffic-congested city. People spent the equivalent of 215 episodes of Game of Thrones stuck in gridlock every year.

This is the story of how a small group of citizens—armed with little more than determination, data, and democratic ideals—fought for a decade to bring commuter rail to India's technology capital.

It's a field guide for anyone who wants to make their city better but doesn't know where to start.

About the Author

Sathya Sankaran

Sathya Sankaran is a civic activist, technology professional, and urban mobility advocate based in Bengaluru.

As a founding member of the Namma Railu campaign, he worked tirelessly for over a decade to advocate for commuter rail services. He served as the "Bicycle Mayor" of Bengaluru in 2018 and helped launch initiatives like Cycle Day, which ran for over six years.

With degrees in Computer Science and Public Policy, Sathya has spent over two decades working at the intersection of technology, governance, and citizen engagement.

Break the Block is his first book.
