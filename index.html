About the Author

Sathya Sankaran

Sathya Sankaran is a civic activist, technology professional, urban mobility advocate, and podcast host based in Bengaluru.

In 2007, Sathya discovered a second calling: advocating for better urban infrastructure and sustainable transportation in his beloved hometown. As a founding member of the Namma Railu campaign, he worked tirelessly for over a decade to bring commuter rail services to Bengaluru. His efforts in sustainable urban mobility extended far beyond rail advocacy.

As the "Bicycle Mayor" of Bengaluru‚Äîa citizen leadership role focused on promoting cycling infrastructure‚ÄîSathya helped launch Cycle Day, India's longest-running community-led open streets event, in partnership with the Government of Karnataka.

During the COVID-19 lockdown in India, his Relief Riders initiative delivered groceries and medicines to those in need. The grassroots movement spread across 12 cities with 600+ volunteer riders, earning the UN World Bicycle Day award and receiving Nobel Peace Prize nominations from supporters worldwide.

Host of The OoruLabs Podcast

Sathya hosts The OoruLabs Podcast, India's premier urban affairs podcast exploring cities, governance, infrastructure, and civic engagement. Through in-depth conversations with policy makers, activists, and urban experts, the podcast examines what makes cities work‚Äîand how citizens can make them better.
