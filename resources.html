2009
The Beginning
Seeds of Change
  • Praja.in platform established for civic engagement
  • Anonymous online discussions about commuter rail begin
  • Campaign team starts forming
  • Initial research and data gathering
Foundation Phase
2010
Building the Case
Research & First Resistance
  • Comprehensive commuter rail report prepared
  • Team collaboration across three continents
  • Historical research on Indian Railways
  • First setback: BMRCL MD calls proposal "grandiose"
First Block Appears
2011
Navigating the Maze
Government Meetings Begin
  • First meeting inside Vidhana Soudha
  • October 19, 2011: RTI filed to uncover railway track availability in Karnataka
  • RTI response reveals detailed status of 14 railway lines, costs, and timelines
  • Learning to navigate multiple government levels
  • Created "BRVC" concept to push idea forward
  • Using transparency laws to gather data for advocacy
  • Persistent follow-ups despite slow responses
2012-2014
The Long Middle
Years of Persistence
  • Multiple meetings with various officials
  • Email chains and bureaucratic ping-pong
  • Contradictory signals from government
  • Internal team tensions and frustrations
  • Sustained advocacy despite setbacks
Persistence Phase
2015-2016
Finding Champions
The Corridors Open
  • Allies emerge inside government system
  • Technical feasibility discussions become serious
  • Shifting from "if" to "how"
  • Building credibility through persistent advocacy
2017
Railway Minister's Letter
Federal Government Engagement
  • November 20, 2017: Railway Minister Piyush Goyal writes to Karnataka CM Siddaramaiah
  • Letter suggests using K-RIDE as State JV company for suburban rail
  • Federal government enters correspondence with Karnataka
  • Request for Karnataka to expedite State Government approval
  • Railway Ministry offers to assess infrastructure needs for next 5-10 years
Federal Buy-In
2018
Strategic Patience
Navigating Approvals
  • Following up on Railway Minister's 2017 letter
  • Political changes affect timeline
  • Strategic decisions: when to push vs. wait
  • Managing hope and frustration within team
2019
The Reckoning
Crisis Point
  • Bengaluru becomes world's most traffic-congested city
  • 243 hours/year stuck in traffic per commuter
  • Public pressure for solutions intensifies
  • Media attention increases
  • Government acknowledgment grows
Urgency Peaks
2020
Victory
Breaking Through the Block
  • January-September: Seven months of silence
  • October 8: Union Cabinet approval announced!
  • Unexpected breakthrough after decade-long effort
  • Suburban rail network approved
  • Proof that citizen advocacy works
Cabinet Approval
2021-2024
Vindication
The Proof
  • First suburban rail services begin operation
  • 295% occupancy proves demand was real
  • Success validates decade of advocacy
  • Model for other cities and campaigns
  • Book manuscript completed documenting the journey
Success
December 2025
Sharing the Lessons
Website Launch
  • Official website launched at breaktheblock.in
  • 90 lessons from the campaign made freely available online
  • Free chapter previews accessible to all readers
  • Interactive timeline and resources published
  • Campaign documents archived for future activists
  • Community discussions enabled for knowledge sharing
